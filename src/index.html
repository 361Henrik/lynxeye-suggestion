The conversation so far

Situation & ambition

The short version: Lynxeye has the judgment and demand. The work is to make that judgment easier to reuse without lowering the standard.

Situation

Lynxeye is a premium consultancy of around 70 people, with more attractive work than the team can easily absorb. The opportunity is real, but capacity is constrained.

AI use already exists, but much of it is still individual and scattered. Prompts disappear, context resets, best practice stays private, and every project risks starting from scratch.

The senior judgment that defines Lynxeye is still the bottleneck. Until it is structured into reusable ways of working, AI can help individuals but it will not compound into company capability.

Ambition

Scale and orchestrate with AI without losing what makes Lynxeye premium. The goal is not to use AI more. The goal is to reduce daily friction, grow capacity, and keep senior judgment leading.

Turn senior expertise into reusable practice. Give people better starting points. Let the first playbooks emerge from real client work rather than from a separate training exercise.

When Henrik leaves, the capability stays. The playbooks are owned by Lynxeye. The methods belong to Lynxeye. The IP is built inside the firm.

Friction removed first

Escape Velocity starts in the ordinary workday. Before the upside can compound, people need a setup where the basics stop slowing them down.

  • Right folders and documents are connected.
  • People know where to save work.
  • Voice input is available where useful.
  • Email and documents move faster.
  • Best practice from others is visible.
  • Reusable context replaces project setup.

The journey

Phase 1 is the collaboration suggested here: escape from the current way of working. Phase 2 is where the upside starts to compound. Phase 3 is the strategic frontier beyond this engagement.

Most companies fail to scale with AI because the new way of working is rougher than the old one at the start. Setup is scattered, context is missing, people type when speaking would be faster, folders are hard to find, and private prompts never become shared practice.

Phase 1 is designed to escape the old working habits. It removes day-to-day friction, builds the first capability, and proves the new way through live client case work. Phase 2 and Phase 3 are context, not proposed scope.

Ping-pong vs. orchestration

How Lynxeye employees interact with AI today, and what changes when the practice becomes orchestrated.

Most people start with AI in ping-pong mode: ask a question, get an answer, ask another, get another. It feels productive in the moment. It does not compound. The prompts disappear. The context resets. Next week, the wheel is reinvented.

Orchestration is what happens when AI use stops being individual and starts being systemic. Playbooks accumulate. Senior expertise gets structured. AI works alongside the team, not in parallel to it. The same person doing the same client work produces dramatically more, with quality preserved.

The difference is not the tool. The difference is the practice. The same AI tools, Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini, produce ad hoc results for one person and orchestrated capacity for another.

One workflow, two ways

A proposal draft for a financial services client. Today on the left, once the work is orchestrated on the right.

Today · Ping-pong mode

Senior strategist opens a blank deck. AI is used in separate chat sessions for industry context, frameworks, and draft language. The useful pieces are copied, pasted, reformatted, and reviewed, but the work remains fragmented. Each round restarts with partial memory.

Output: one-off draft.

Orchestrated

Strategist opens the Lynxeye Proposal Playbook. Client context, sector knowledge, tone, and quality expectations are already structured. The strategist briefs, challenges, reviews, and refines the output. What works goes back into the playbook for the next team.

Output: reusable working asset.

Why most companies do not escape

Four forces pull teams back into ping-pong mode. Prompts disappear, good thinking happens in chat windows and vanishes. Knowledge stays scattered, senior expertise lives in heads, decks, and habits. Best practices do not scale, one person figures something out and the other 69 do not benefit. Every project starts from scratch, no playbooks, no shared assets, no compounding.

The cost of waiting compounds. Every quarter Lynxeye stays in ping-pong mode, the gap widens between Lynxeye and the firms that escaped earlier. The intelligence is already inside Lynxeye. AI does not create it. AI activates it. The question is when.
