# Personas reading guide

A reference for who at Lynxeye reads what part of the proposal, used in the email companion to Christian.

The email instructs Christian (or whoever forwards the link) which parts of the proposal will land best for which colleague.

## The personas

### Christian — the buyer
**Senior partner. Has been Henrik's primary contact. Has seen the Dancing with Intelligence deck.**

**Recommended reading path:**
- Hero (10 seconds)
- Panel 4 (My Suggestion for Collaboration) — the substance
- Panel 1 (Situation & Ambition) for the framing
- Panel 2 (Back of the Envelope) if interested in the math
- Modal 2 (About Henrik) if a refresher is useful

**What he's looking for:** Confirmation that the proposal reflects what they've been discussing. A clear shape for Phase 1 to bring to the partner team. Something he can comfortably forward.

**Time budget:** 10-15 minutes for the deep read. 3 minutes for the scan.

### Niklas — the CEO
**Decision authority. Hasn't been in the conversations directly.**

**Recommended reading path:**
- Hero (the divergence visual carries the argument in 5 seconds)
- Panel 4 (My Suggestion for Collaboration) — the locked core and the timeline
- Panel 1 (Situation & Ambition) — confirms Henrik understands Lynxeye
- Modal 1 (Ping-pong vs. orchestration) — if he wants the "why this matters" frame

**What he's looking for:** Strategic shape. What does Lynxeye commit to? What's the timeline? Who's involved? What's the risk?

**Time budget:** 5-7 minutes. Will scan, not read carefully on first pass.

### Johan and Viking — the internal AI team
**Already doing meaningful AI work. Potential allies or quiet obstacles depending on framing.**

**Recommended reading path:**
- Hero (orientation)
- Panel 4, "How we work" tab — specifically the "Built on what Johan and Viking are doing" section
- Panel 4, locked core item 2 (Kickoff working session) and item 9 (First playbooks)
- Modal 2 (About Henrik) — Henrik's three commitments, especially "the intelligence is already there"

**What they're looking for:** Confirmation that Henrik respects what they've already built. Clarity on their role in Phase 1 (active participants, not bypassed). Assurance that the playbooks stay Lynxeye-owned.

**Critical:** The "How we work" tab is politically load-bearing. If Johan and Viking feel respected and extended, they become allies. If they feel replaced, they become quiet obstacles.

**Time budget:** 10 minutes. Will read the "How we work" content carefully.

### The skeptical senior partner — unnamed persona
**Hasn't been part of the conversations. Skeptical of AI hype generally. Worried about quality drift, hiring alternatives, real ROI.**

**Recommended reading path:**
- Panel 2 (Back of the Envelope) — the math
- The hiring alternative inside Panel 2 — the budget comparison
- Panel 4, locked core item 10 (Light quality guardrails) — the work-slop prevention
- Panel 4, locked core items 11-13 — the outcomes Lynxeye gets

**What they're looking for:** Honest math. Acknowledgment of risks. Specific deliverables, not vague promises. Evidence that Henrik has thought about what could go wrong.

**Critical:** This persona will scrutinize the simulator. The (i) info indicators, the sources, and the "what we don't know yet" callout are designed for this reader.

**Time budget:** 5 minutes of skeptical scanning. Will look for things to push back on.

### The wider partner team
**Won't read deeply. Will form an impression from headlines and visuals.**

**Recommended reading path:**
- Hero
- The four navigator cards (the summaries)
- Modal 1 (Ping-pong vs. orchestration) for the framing

**What they're looking for:** Whether this feels like a serious proposal or a vendor pitch. Whether Christian's recommendation seems considered.

**Time budget:** 90 seconds. The hero and the navigator cards do most of the work.

## How this gets used in the email

In the email companion, this guide is condensed to a single sentence or two per persona:

> A reading guide if it helps:
> - **For you, Christian:** The full proposal. Start at Panel 4 (My suggestion for collaboration) if you want the substance immediately.
> - **For Niklas:** The hero plus Panel 4. Five minutes for the strategic shape.
> - **For Johan and Viking:** Panel 4, especially the "How we work" tab. The integration with what they're already doing is explicit and important.
> - **For the skeptical partner:** Panel 2 (Back of the envelope), then the hiring alternative inside it. The math.

The full detail in this file stays as reference for any future iteration of the email.
