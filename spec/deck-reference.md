# Deck reference — Dancing with Intelligence

This document tracks what was pulled from Henrik's "Dancing with Intelligence — Moving on from Ping Pong" deck (May 2026, the deck Christian has already seen) into the Project Accelerate Lynxeye proposal — and what was deliberately retired.

## Context

The Dancing with Intelligence deck is the shared artifact Christian was presented with in conversation. It established:
- The "ping-pong vs orchestration" framing
- The tagline "Human judgment leads. AI accelerates. Playbooks scale."
- A visual language of cream backgrounds, gold accents, hand-drawn touches
- A series of metaphors and visuals around AI orchestration in consulting firms

The proposal builds on this shared context. It does NOT reintroduce concepts from scratch — Christian has seen them. Instead, it extends and applies them to Lynxeye specifically.

## What was pulled in (used in the proposal)

### Concepts and language

1. **"Ping-pong vs. orchestration"** — the core framing. Lives in Modal 1 and throughout Panel 1's situation prose.
2. **The tagline** — used verbatim in the hero and possibly the closing context.
3. **"The intelligence is already inside Lynxeye"** — used in About Henrik modal as the philosophical commitment.
4. **The four forces (prompts disappear, knowledge stays scattered, best practices don't scale, every project starts from scratch)** — pulled into Modal 1.
5. **The escape velocity metaphor** — Phase 1 = Escape Velocity. Used in Panel 3 and as the phase naming convention.
6. **"Train through the work" / live cases beat generic training** — used in Panel 4's "How we work" tab.
7. **"Humans stay in charge"** — used in About Henrik modal as one of the three commitments.
8. **"The live-case loop"** — used in Panel 4's "How we work" tab as a visual.

### Visuals

Four visuals were redrawn in editorial daylight palette (cream `#F6F3EE`, gold `#C9A962`, brand green `#2E6B54`) — never imported as raster from the deck:

1. **The 24-month divergence curve** → became **the orchestrating-vs-ping-pong divergence** in the hero. No month markers (per Henrik's decision). Two paths from the same starting point.

2. **The flowing cover-page curves** → became the **hero background curves** behind the elevator pitch. Quietly references the deck.

3. **The fluency ladder (5 steps)** → became the **three-phase rising borders** in Panel 3. Three phases (Escape Velocity → Ascent → New Frontiers) instead of five fluency steps. Henrik's decision to keep three-phase naming.

4. **The "how the method scales" loop** → became the **live-case loop SVG** in Panel 4. Five-step circular flow with Steps 3 and 4 (Review & Refine, Playbook Update) filled in gold to mark the leverage points. Center caption: "WHERE THE LEVERAGE LIVES."

## What was deliberately retired (NOT used in the proposal)

These deck visuals were considered and deliberately not pulled through. Reasons noted so they don't accidentally come back:

1. **The chaotic ping-pong fan visual** — too dramatic for the proposal's calm editorial register. Replaced by the cleaner divergence curve.

2. **The Human↔AI waveform** — the side-by-side comparison in Modal 1 does this work in prose form.

3. **The tangle-to-bullseye visual ("focus effort on strategic pursuits")** — overlaps conceptually with the divergence curve. Cutting one was needed; this one went.

4. **The before/after timeline ("From blank page to structured proof")** — the side-by-side workflow comparison in Modal 1 covers the same argument.

5. **The "Humans stay in charge" two-column table** — the three commitments in the About Henrik modal carry this content.

6. **The proposal pipeline (Setup → Research → Synthesis → Deliverable → Review) with gold-highlighted middle steps** — too operationally specific. The high-level live-case loop already shows where the leverage lives.

7. **The "Why live cases beat generic training" comparison** — Panel 4's "How we work" tab covers this in prose.

8. **The "Turn tacit knowledge into company infrastructure" funnel** — Core deliverable #9 (First Lynxeye playbooks) handles this in prose.

9. **The Live Case Sprint Pilot Steps timeline (with Porsche reference)** — too operationally specific. Belongs in a later document (e.g., a Phase 1 kickoff deck), not the proposal.

10. **All Porsche references** — Lynxeye has no Porsche relationship. Replace any with "a dream client of Lynxeye's choosing" or generic framing.

## What was extended or invented

These concepts were not in the deck but extend it logically for the proposal:

1. **The use-case map / opportunity surface as a core deliverable** — extends the orchestration concept to "the work also produces strategic intelligence about where AI fits next." This is a Henrik-flagged addition that didn't exist in the deck.

2. **The hiring alternative comparison** — extends the deck's productivity argument to the actual budget conversation (hire 3 people vs. orchestrate). Christian shared the "short 3 finance people" detail in conversation; this is built on that.

3. **The Phase 2 recommendation document as a core deliverable** — the deck implies a journey but doesn't name the artifact that bridges Phase 1 to Phase 2. This is new.

4. **The engagement charter / working agreement** — the deck doesn't address operating mechanics. This is a Henrik addition for premium consultancy operating clarity.

5. **The documented setup guide for the rest of the firm** — implicit in the deck's scaling argument; made explicit as a deliverable.

## Visual continuity rules

To maintain continuity with the deck Christian has already seen:

- **Same color palette family** (cream, gold, brand green) but in editorial daylight rather than deck-style dark slate. Cream `#F6F3EE` instead of deck's dark `#2A2D2C`. Gold `#C9A962` shared.
- **Hand-drawn quality preserved** in the divergence curve through anchor dots and confident stroke weight.
- **Typography:** the deck used a serif for some headlines. The proposal uses Lexend throughout (sans-serif). This is a deliberate shift — premium editorial documents lean sans-serif in 2026.
- **The tagline appears verbatim** in both deck and proposal. Single source of identity continuity.

## Things the deck did better that we should aspire to

For Claude Code's awareness during the build:

- **The deck's hand-drawn touches** (especially the chaotic ping-pong fan and the tangle visual) gave it personality. The proposal is more restrained. Where possible, preserve hand-drawn quality in visuals — confidence in line weight, anchor dots, organic curves over geometric ones.
- **The deck's rhythm of one-idea-per-slide.** The proposal should preserve some of this discipline: each panel makes one core argument, with details available on click.
- **The deck's deliberate use of white space.** Premium documents breathe. Don't fill the page.
